2020 Top 10 CO2 Emissions

In 2020, China's total carbon dioxide emissions will be 11.68 billion tons, ranking first in the world. It was followed by the United States (4.535 billion tons) and India (2.412 billion tons). It's worth noting, however, that these are also the three most populous countries in the world, so their high ranking among carbon dioxide emitters - which is mostly released through transport networks and electricity production - can largely be attributed to their higher total populations.







CO2 Emissions In the USA (2001-2021)

As the second largest emitter of carbon dioxide in the United States, the control of carbon emissions is also crucial.

This chart shows that the carbon dioxide emissions in the United States are decreasing year by year, and there is a small rebound in 2021, which is a good trend, because the control of carbon dioxide can reduce the greenhouse effect and achieve sustainable development.







CO2 emissions by fuel type



Carbon dioxide is mainly caused by the burning of fuels, including coal, oil, gas, cement, flaring, and other industries. In order from most to least carbon dioxide emissions, coal, oil and gas are the main ones.
